About M4C

As a trained musician and mindfulness teacher I’ve spent the last decade trying to understand how mindfulness can allow creatives not only to perform better but to become more resilient in facing the challenges presented on the creative path.

When we shift from struggling against present reality to curious inquiry into its feeling tone/mood/mental landscape/fingerprint/body-print we practice the simple power of mindfulness. With a more refined attention and a different attitude to experience we can begin to see ourselves (and the ways in which we are obstructing ourselves) more clearly…

With mindfulness we begin the intimately personal process of unbinding from our perceived attachments and limitations, not through changing who we are but through understanding who we are more deeply

When we practice mindfulness we develop the skills of:

  • Self-compassion
  • Curiosity
  • Equanimity or Mental Balance
  • Concentration
  • Mental Clarity/Refinement
  • Ease and noticing ease
